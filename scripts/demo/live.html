Auto-router validation · >4-speaker fallback path (measured 2026-06-04)

When transcription_config.speaker_diarization_config.max_speakers > 4, the auto-multispeaker meta-engine routes to nemotron-native + sortformer-streaming v2.1 instead of multitalker. Same SCOTUS oral-argument audio streamed at max_speakers=12 to validate the routing fires correctly and the fallback handles a real high-speaker-count hint without breaking.

Test conditionRoute takenSpeakers · ref / hypConfusionTTFT
SCOTUS @ max_speakers=4Multitalker3 / 3 0.4 %~2.5 s
SCOTUS @ max_speakers=12Nemotron + Sortformer-streaming3 / 3 13.6 %~17 s cold / ~2 s warm

Validates routing end-to-end. Same audio (3 real speakers — Roberts, Thomas, Waxman) dispatched to two different engine stacks based on max_speakers. Both identify the correct speaker count without hallucinating extras. max_speakers has two roles: (1) the router picks the engine — at ≤4 it routes to Multitalker, at >4 to the Nemotron + Sortformer-streaming fallback; (2) within Multitalker, the verifier enforces max_speakers as a hard cap on identity allocation — S{max_speakers+1} cannot appear, voice variation force-routes to one of the established identities. For a known 2-attendee deposition, set max_speakers=2 and S1/S2 are guaranteed. Multitalker wins on confusion% on its native ≤4 turf (0.4 vs 13.6); fallback is the safety net for sessions that exceed the 4-channel architecture (multi-party depos, mediations, oral arguments).

Trade-off documented: first >4-spk session per container has a ~17 s cold-load to bring up the fallback model (subsequent sessions ~2 s). Multitalker stays the default for solo/standard depositions; fallback kicks in only when the session shape requires it.

Hardware floor story. Speechmatics on-prem documents 16 GB VRAM as a hard minimum — the enhanced model alone consumes ~14–15 GB just to load, and the container refuses to start below that. Adding pyannote on top realistically requires more VRAM. Our M2 stack drops the floor: Multitalker fits in any 8 GB consumer GPU; CPU-only Parakeet-ONNX runs in ~2 GB RAM on any modern laptop with no GPU at all. That's a $4 k Alienware-class laptop spec → <$1000 laptop, plus a per-courthouse shared-server option on a sub-$1500 consumer GPU box.

Post-process layers · selectable on top of any engine

Two optional layers stack on top of the engine + diarizer to clean up the output. Toggle them on/off from the Playground tab to A-B compare.

LayerDefaultWhat it doesCost
Speaker-label smoother
built into Multitalker · intra-chunk		always onHysteresis + min-speaker-duration post-process. Words attributed to a speaker with <500 ms sustained activity in the surrounding ±600 ms window get reassigned to the dominant speaker in that window. Eliminates phantom S3/S4 ghosts from diarizer single-frame flicker. Per Streaming Sortformer paper (arXiv 2507.18446) §IV.~0.9 s buffer latency
TitaNet speaker verifier
built into Multitalker · multi-prototype + s-norm + max_speakers cap		always onEach dominant-channel chunk runs through NVIDIA speakerverification_en_titanet_large (192-dim x-vector embeddings, verification-trained on VoxCeleb). Multi-prototype centroids (K=3 looks per identity) handle voice variation; new-channel emissions are eager-classified via adaptive s-norm against a trial-side cohort (in-session impostors + an 84-embedding static backstop) with raw_sim > 0.80 AND z > 2.5 as the merge gate. max_speakers from transcription_config acts as a hard cap on identity allocation — once N identities are established, voice variation force-routes to the closest existing identity rather than spawning S{N+1}. NVIDIA's canonical multitalker recipe ships no verification layer; this is bespoke engineering for label stability under drift, pause-recovery, and known-attendee sessions.~95 MB GPU-resident · ~30 ms per chunk
Punctuation post-process
DistilBERT or passthrough		distilbert (default)NVIDIA punctuation_en_distilbert model (110 MB, CC-BY-4.0) restores periods, commas, question marks + capitalization on the final words list. Same two-stage cascade pattern Speechmatics / AssemblyAI / Deepgram use. Switch to passthrough in the Playground dropdown to A/B compare raw vs punctuated output.~10 ms GPU / ~50 ms CPU per emission

Why these are layered, not baked in: court reporters editing live want different things than batch reviewers. Speaker stability runs in two complementary layers — the smoother handles single-frame flicker inside a chunk; TitaNet handles drift across chunks. Punctuation defaults on because legal transcripts read like a court reporter's QA inbox without it — switch to passthrough in the Playground dropdown to see the raw engine output.

Methodology disclaimers. n=1 per cell. No multi-seed variance. Untuned defaults (no KenLM / beam / vocab biasing).
